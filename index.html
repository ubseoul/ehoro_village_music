The Sushi Formula

4 ingredients per beat, every time

Philosophy: Just like sushi = rice + fish + nori + wasabi, your beats = 4 core elements. Simple. Minimal. Elegant. Timeless.

ü•Å INGREDIENT 1: RHYTHM

Primary (60%): 60%
Lo-Fi Boom Bap
  • Warm, dusty, soft (70-85 BPM)
  • Vinyl crackle integrated
  • Source: Splice "lo-fi boom bap loops"
Secondary (25%): 25%
DnB/Breakbeat
  • Amen break (160-180 BPM, half-time)
  • Emotional, not aggressive
  • Source: Splice "drum & bass"
Tertiary (15%): 15%
Minimal/No Drums
  • Light percussion or ambient
  • For winter signature songs
  • Let space and silence speak
NON-NEGOTIABLE: Drums ALWAYS softer than vocals. Never overpowering. Felt more than heard.

üéπ INGREDIENT 2: HARMONY

Primary (60%): 60%
Rhodes Electric Piano
  • Warm, nostalgic, lo-fi aesthetic
  • Must appear in EVERY song
  • Source: Splice "Rhodes chord loops"
  • Jazzy voicings (Maj7, Min7, Min9)
Secondary (25%): 25%
Jazz Piano
  • Sophisticated, emotional
  • For winter ambient songs
  • Complex voicings, minimal
  • Source: Splice "jazz piano"
Tertiary (15%): 15%
Synth Pads
  • Warm analog or cold digital
  • For video game aesthetic tracks
  • Source: Splice "ambient pads"
NON-NEGOTIABLE: Rhodes foundation is MANDATORY (your signature). Keep it simple (4-8 chords max).

üîä INGREDIENT 3: LOW END

Primary (90%): 90%
Clean Sub Bass
  • Pure sine wave, felt not heard
  • Below 60Hz, just root notes
  • Minimal movement, max weight
  • Program yourself
Secondary (10%): 10%
Warm Synth Bass
  • Only for tropical/summer songs
  • 60-250Hz range, more melodic
  • Source: Splice "warm bass loops"
NON-NEGOTIABLE: Sub bass ALWAYS clean, NEVER distorted. Felt in chest, not speakers.

‚ú® INGREDIENT 4: TEXTURE

Primary (50%): 50%
Video Game Sounds
  • 8-bit bleeps, retro game SFX
  • ONE per song (not both types)
  • Nostalgic for Gen Z childhood
  • Source: Freesound.org "8-bit"
Secondary (30%): 30%
Japanese Instruments
  • Temple bells (1 hit per 8 bars MAX)
  • Koto (tiny flourishes)
  • Shakuhachi (breathy flute)
  • Source: Splice "Japanese instruments"
Tertiary (20%): 20%
Vinyl/Nature Ambience
  • Vinyl crackle (10-15% volume)
  • Rain, wind, snow (winter songs)
  • Ocean waves (tropical songs)
  • Source: Freesound.org, BBC SFX
NON-NEGOTIABLE: Textures feel like MEMORIES. Subtle, never competing with vocals. ONE texture per song.

The Beat-Making Workflow

PREP (Monthly - 1-2 hours)
Download 20-30 samples from Splice. Organize into folders: DRUMS, CHORDS, MELODIES, TEXTURES, BASS
DAY 1: Gather (30 min)
Pick 1 drum loop, 1 chord progression, 1-2 textures. Drop into DAW.
DAY 2: Combine (45 min)
Arrange drums, layer chords, program sub bass (10 min), add textures, create dynamics
DAY 3: Mix (45 min)
Level balance, EQ, compression, saturation, reverb, add vinyl crackle, export
The Quality Tests:
  • Winter Test: Cold, contemplative, peaceful?
  • Sushi Test: 4 ingredients clear? Not cluttered?
  • France Test: Would Paris at 2am understand?
